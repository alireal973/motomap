What's New in MotoMap

Algorithm changes, formula derivations, and version history. All LaTeX formulas rendered in dark theme.

v0.5.0 April 11, 2026 Latest Breaking

Complete rewrite of the speed estimation engine. The flat multiplier model has been replaced with a five-layer system grounded in the Highway Capacity Manual (HCM), BPR volume-delay function, and motorcycle lane-filtering research. The algorithm is now real-time ready -- when a traffic feed populates per-edge V/C ratios, congestion-adjusted routing activates automatically.

New Features
  • BPR Volume-Delay Function -- Traffic congestion modeled via the Bureau of Public Roads (1964) function with per-road-class alpha and beta parameters.
    BPR Link Cost Function
    $$ t(V) = t_{ff} \cdot \left[1 + \alpha \cdot \left(\frac{V}{C}\right)^{\beta}\right] $$

    where $V$ = traffic volume (veh/h), $C$ = practical capacity, $t_{ff}$ = free-flow travel time, $\alpha \in [0.15, 0.50]$, $\beta \in [3.0, 4.0]$ per road class.

  • Motorcycle Lane-Filtering Speed Model -- When congestion exceeds $V/C > 0.60$ and the road has $\geq 2$ forward lanes, motorcycles receive a speed bonus over car traffic based on UC Berkeley SafeTREC research.
    Lane-Filtering Speed
    $$ V_{moto} = \begin{cases} V_{car} + 5 & n_{lane} = 1 \\ V_{car} + 15 & n_{lane} = 2 \\ V_{car} + 20 & n_{lane} \geq 3 \end{cases} $$

    Activated only when $V/C > 0.60$. Floor: $V_{moto} \geq 15$ km/h.

  • Road-Class Free-Flow Speed Factors -- Each highway type now has its own fraction of posted speed achievable under free-flow conditions, following HCM 6th Ed. Chapter 23 methodology.
    Effective Free-Flow Speed
    $$ V_{ff} = V_{posted} \times f_{class} \times f_{surface} \times f_{grade} \times f_{global} $$
  • Surface-Type Speed Reduction -- 20 OSM surface types mapped to motorcycle-specific speed factors. Gravel ($f = 0.45$), cobblestone ($f = 0.55$), and mud ($f = 0.15$) dramatically reduce speed compared to asphalt ($f = 1.0$).
  • Grade Kinematic Speed Reduction -- Steep grades now reduce the base speed, not just the post-hoc cost. Follows simplified HCM Exhibit 23-8:
    Grade Factor
    $$ f_{grade} = \max\!\left(0.40,\; 1.0 - 0.35 \times |g|\right) $$

    A 10% grade ($|g| = 0.10$) reduces free-flow speed by 3.5%. A 15% grade reduces it by 5.25%. Floor at 40% of posted speed.

  • Per-Class Intersection Delay -- Motorways: 0 s, primaries: 8 s, residential: 3 s. Replaces the previous flat 2 s on every edge. Based on HCM Chapter 19 control-delay methodology.
  • Real-Time Traffic Integration Path -- V/C ratio can come from edge_data["vc_ratio"] (live feed), MOTOMAP_VC_RATIO env var (testing), or statistical peak-hour defaults (planning mode). The architecture is plug-and-play for IBB Open Data, Google Roads API, TomTom, or HERE feeds.
  • Peak-Hour Statistical V/C Ratios -- When no live traffic is available, the model uses typical Turkish urban peak V/C ratios per road class (e.g., motorway 0.75, primary 0.85, residential 0.40).
Changed
  • compute_edge_travel_time() -- Completely rewritten. Now calls _effective_speed_kmh() (5-layer model) and _intersection_delay() instead of the old maxspeed * speed_factor / 3.6 + 2.0.
  • RoutingAlgorithmProfile -- Added 7 new fields: bpr_alpha, bpr_beta, road_capacity_per_lane, peak_hour_vc_ratio, lane_filter_speed_bonus, lane_filter_vc_threshold, lane_filter_min_speed_kmh.
  • RuntimeCalibration -- Added vc_ratio_override field, populated from MOTOMAP_VC_RATIO env var.
  • _graph_cache_token() -- Now includes surface in the hash, so surface tag edits properly invalidate caches.
Fixed
  • CC restriction expanded to 125 cc -- Turkish law (Karayollari Trafik Yonetmeligi, Madde 38) and EU Directive 2006/126/EC prohibit motorcycles below 125 cc from motorways and expressways. Previously only $\leq 50$ cc were restricted.
    Highway Restriction Penalty
    $$ C_{highway}(e) = \begin{cases} 10^6 & CC < 125 \text{ and } e \in \{\text{motorway, trunk}\} \\ 1.0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
  • Surface type was computed but never used -- fill_edge_defaults() wrote surface to every edge, but compute_edge_travel_time() never read it. Now integrated as $f_{surface}$ in the speed model.
  • Flat speed factor produced absurd speeds -- Motorway at 120 km/h was reduced to 66 km/h (same ratio as living_street 20 → 11 km/h). Now each road class has its own free-flow factor.
  • Cache invalidation missed surface changes -- Editing an edge's surface tag did not invalidate cached travel times.
Breaking Changes
  • Travel time values changed -- All edge travel times differ from v0.4.0 due to the new multi-factor speed model. Cached route benchmarks and comparison baselines must be regenerated.
  • Motorcycles 51--124 cc are now banned from motorways -- Routes for these displacements will avoid motorways and expressways. Previously only $\leq 50$ cc were restricted.

v0.4.0 March 6, 2026

Initial public release of the motorcycle routing engine. Bidirectional Dijkstra search, toll-aware routing, curvature and risk analysis, three riding modes, and engine displacement (CC) penalties.

Highlights
  • Bidirectional Dijkstra with precomputed indexed adjacency for fast repeated queries.
  • Three riding modes: Standart (commute), Viraj Keyfi (curvy), Guvenli (safe).
  • Curvature analysis via vectorized angle computation with fun/danger/hairpin classification.
    Curvature Score (chunk-normalized)
    $$ \kappa = \text{mean}\!\left(\frac{fun\_ratio_i}{1 + danger\_ratio_i}\right) $$
  • Engine displacement penalties -- Three-tier CC-aware slope multiplier.
    Grade Penalty (CC-aware)
    $$ C_{grade}(e) = \begin{cases} 6.0 & CC \leq 50,\; |g| > 0.12 \\ 2.0 & CC < 250,\; |g| > 0.12 \\ 1.35 & CC \geq 250,\; |g| > 0.14 \\ 1.0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
  • Toll-aware routing -- Computes both toll-allowed and toll-free alternatives in one call.
  • Elevation fallback chain -- Google API → OpenTopo → degrade mode (elevation = 0).
  • 1000 randomized shortest-path tests verifying equivalence with NetworkX.
