Features

Built for Motorcycle Riders

Beyond standard navigation. Curvature quality, road surface, grade analysis, and traffic-flow-theory-based speed estimation.

Five-Layer Speed Model

Road-class free-flow factors, BPR volume-delay congestion, surface-type reduction, grade kinematic slowdown, and motorcycle lane-filtering. Grounded in HCM Chapter 23 and BPR (1964).

Curvature Analysis

Vectorized angle computation classifies every segment as fun, dangerous, or hairpin for ride quality scoring.

Weather Integration

Real-time weather along your route. Adjust plans before you ride, not during.

Surface Awareness

20 surface types from asphalt to gravel to mud, each with motorcycle-specific speed factors from field research.

Real-Time Ready Architecture

BPR congestion model activates automatically when live V/C ratios are available from traffic feeds (IBB Open Data, Google, TomTom, HERE). Without live data, statistical peak-hour defaults provide realistic estimates.

How It Works

Three Steps to Ride

Pick your destination, choose your style, and let the algorithm handle the rest.

01

Set Your Destination

Drop pins on the map or search by name. MotoMap downloads the road network from OpenStreetMap automatically.

02

Choose a Riding Mode

Sport optimizes for speed. Twisty maximizes curves. Safe avoids steep grades and poor surfaces.

03

Hit the Road

View your route on the map with elevation profile, weather forecast, and real-time traffic conditions.

Riding Modes

A Route for Every Rider

Sport

Fastest path optimized for speed and efficiency. Direct highways and smooth roads.

Twisty

Maximum curvature and riding fun. Seeks out mountain passes, coastal bends, and winding valleys.

Safe

Avoids steep grades, unpaved surfaces, and hazardous segments. Prioritizes well-maintained roads.
